Why not standard Wald? The standard Wald formula (p ± z·√(p(1-p)/n)) produces intervals that are too narrow and can fall outside [0,1] at small n. Adjusted-Wald adds 2 pseudo-successes and 2 pseudo-failures before computing, which consistently outperforms Wald at n < 40. For 0% or 100% outcomes, LaPlace (add 1 success and 2 to n) is used instead.
